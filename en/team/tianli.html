Li Tian

Li Tian
Institute of Geographic Sciences and Natural Resources Research,
Chinese Academy of Sciences
Associate Research Fellow

Biography

Li Tian, female, is an associate researcher at the Institute of Geographic Sciences and Resources, Chinese Academy of Sciences (IGSR, CAS), is a Master's supervisor, and has a Doctor of Science degree.

Graduated from Sun Yat-sen University with a Ph.D. degree in 2013, worked as a postdoctoral fellow at the Institute of Geographic Sciences and Resources, Chinese Academy of Sciences from 2013-2015, stayed in the Institute in July 2015, and was promoted to associate researcher in 2018.

Chaired the National Natural Science Youth Foundation of China (41601100) on "Characterization of synergistic changes in vegetation cover and surface short-wave albedo of typical grassland types on the Qinghai-Tibetan Plateau", and participated in the National Key Research and Development Program of China (2016YFC1201302) on the topic of "Modeling of Regional Emergent Biohazardous Events". "(2016YFC1201302); as a key project participant in the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) Youth Fund, "Research on Integrated Mapping and Assessment Methods of Anthropogenic Disturbance in Landscape Based on PSR Model" (41701213); and Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) Strategic Pilot Science and Technology Special Project (Class A): Earth Big Data. The program of Strategic Pilot Science and Technology Program of Chinese Academy of Sciences (Class A): Earth Big Data Science Project (XDA19000000), and so on.

Has co-authored nearly 20 papers, including more than 10 first/corresponding SCI papers. He has authored and co-authored one monograph; two software copyrights.

Research interests

  1. Remote sensing applications for ecosystems
  2. Ecological modeling of land surface processes
  3. Urban Landscape Ecology
